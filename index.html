Hi! Nice to meet you! My name is Daniel, and I'm a software engineer with experience in full-stack engineering, application development, and software testing. Growing up I had always found myself trying to solve problems that were way out of my scope of knowledge. Of course, being the stubborn child that I was, "no" was never an acceptable answer and I would spend hours finding the most ridiculous solutions to seemingly unsolvable problems. Like that time in 1st grade where I tried to solve a rubix cube, and realized that I could break apart the unsolved rubix cube and assemble it back into a perfectly solved one. Or that other time as a kid, where I thought I created an infinite shampoo hack by slowly adding tiny bits of water into a shampoo bottle. If you didn't figure it out by now, I've always been the type of person to tackle any problem, no matter how difficult (or simple) it may seem, and relentessly find creative (sometimes stupid) solutions. This drive eventually led me to Hack Reactor, where I learned that software engineering is a place where problems are endless and the solutions are also... endless! As cheesy as it may sound, it almost felt like I had found my calling in life, and it reminded me of a phrase in Japan called 'ikigai', which is the intersection of what you love, what you're good at, what you can be paid for, and what the world needs. Somehow life brought me to this intersection and I want to share it with the world, so feel free to check out my work below!
